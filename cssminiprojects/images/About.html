Apptizers [ Apptizers ]

Here are five popular appetizers in this restaurant, along with their details

1. Chicken 65

A spicy and tangy deep-fried chicken dish, marinated with yogurt, red chili powder, and curry leaves. Often servedwith a lemon wedge and onion slices.

2. Paneer 65

A vegetarian variation of Chicken 65, made with cubes of paneer (cottage cheese), marinated in spices, and deep-fried to perfection.

Minced mutton mixed with spices, shaped onto skewers, and grilled or roasted. Known for its smoky flavor and soft texture.

Entrees [ Entrees ]

Here’s a detailed description of two popular entrées (one vegetarian and one non-vegetarian) served in this restaurants:

1. Non vegetarian Entree

Hyderabadi Dum Biryani is a culinary masterpiece and a must-try dish in Hyderabad. This royal entrée is made using premium-quality basmati rice, layered with marinated meat (usually chicken or mutton) cooked in yogurt, ginger, garlic paste, and a blend of aromatic spices such as cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves. The dish gets its signature flavor from slow-cooking, or “dum,” where the pot is sealed with dough to trap the steam. The saffron-infused rice is layered alternately with the tender, juicy meat, creating a perfect balance of flavors.

2. Vegetarian Entrée

Bagara Baingan is a classic vegetarian entrée and a staple of Hyderabadi cuisine. This dish features small, tender eggplants cooked in a rich, creamy gravy made with roasted peanuts, sesame seeds, and coconut. Tamarind pulp adds a tangy flavor, while a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, and red chili powder enhances the dish's depth. The eggplants are slit and stuffed with the spice mixture before being simmered in the gravy, resulting in a perfectly balanced dish with a nutty, tangy, and mildly spiced flavor profile.

Desserts [ Desserts ]

1. Chocolate Lava Cake

Chocolate Lava Cake is an iconic and indulgent dessert, perfect for any chocolate lover. It consists of a small, individual chocolate cake with a molten, gooey center that oozes out when sliced. This dessert is made by baking a rich, dark chocolate batter in a ramekin or mold, ensuring the outer edges are cooked to form a solid structure while the inner center remains molten. The cake is typically served warm, ensuring that the luxurious molten chocolate floods out with each spoonful, creating a satisfying contrast of textures. The batter for Chocolate Lava Cake is crafted with premium dark chocolate, butter, eggs, and sugar, with a pinch of vanilla extract to enhance its flavor. The key to making the perfect Lava Cake is ensuring the center remains liquid while the exterior is firm enough to hold its shape. This is achieved through careful timing in the oven, typically baking for just 12-15 minutes at a high temperature

2. Creme brulee

Crème Brûlée is a timeless French dessert that combines simplicity with sophistication. This dessert consists of a smooth, rich custard base made from heavy cream, sugar, vanilla beans, and egg yolks. The custard is slowly baked in individual ramekins in a water bath, ensuring a gentle and even cooking process that results in a delicate texture. Once the custard has set and cooled, it is topped with a thin layer of sugar, which is then caramelized using a blowtorch or under a broiler to create a crisp, golden-brown sugar crust.

Beverages [ Beverages ]

Here are some popular beverages commonly served in restaurants:

1. Coffee

Coffee is a staple in many restaurants, especially after meals. It can be served in various forms, such as espresso, cappuccino, latte, and black coffee. It’s a comforting beverage that pairs well with desserts and is often the finishing touch to a meal, particularly in fine dining establishments.

2. Soft Drinks

Soft drinks like cola, lemonade, or iced tea are some of the most commonly ordered beverages in restaurants. They offer a refreshing and familiar option for diners, especially with casual meals, and are usually available in regular, diet, and caffeine-free variations.

3. Fresh Juices

Freshly squeezed fruit juices, such as orange, apple, or mixed fruit juices, are a healthy and refreshing option. They are often offered as an alternative to sugary sodas or alcoholic beverages, making them popular with health-conscious diners. Fresh juices also pair well with breakfast or light meals. These beverages are essential as they cater to a wide range of tastes and dietary preferences, enhancing the dining experience.
